Game rules

Follow these easy steps when playing games

How to create a lobby

  1. All players are given a life counter set to 6.

Never have i ever

  1. A question/statement is shown on screen.

  2. The participants now have to answer weather the have committed the act before. If so then they need to take a sip of their drink.

  3. Participants are encouraged to share backstories to the event.

  4. Repeat until the participants want to move on to something else.

Meyer

  1. All players are given a life counter set to 6.

  2. Player (1) rolls their dice, and claims a value of the dice, this value does not have to be truthful.

  3. The dice are passed from player (1) the challenger to player (2) the challenged.

  4. Player (2) evaluates if the claim is true or false.

  5. If evaluated to be false the cup is to be raised and checked.

  6. If evaluated to be true player (2) re-rolls the dice and has one of two options

  7. a) Player (2) may re-roll once more, but is not allowed to check the result, and has to claim "That or above", with that being what player (1) claimed.

  8. b) Player (2) may claim a value (which does not have to be truthful) and pass the dice in their cups to the next player.

  9. The next player goes back to step 4.

  10. When a player is caught in a lie, or has a roll of insufficient value the player loses 1 life, if the insufficient roll or lie is tied to a roll of the value of 'Meyer' then the player loses 2 lives.

  11. Once a player loses a life the player is set to player (1) and continues from step 2.

  12. This goes on until only one player is left, whom has then lost.

Thinking box

  1. All players are given a set amount of dice (usually between 4 or 6)

  2. The goal is to get rid of one's dice.

  3. Each participant shakes their cups, with their dice inside.

  4. The first player claims the presence of a value of all the participants dice combined.

  5. The next player decides if they believe the claim to be true.

  6. If yes, then the player must come up with a greater value believed to be present.

  7. If no, all players lift their cups and the dice are to be counted.

  8. The player who is wrong in the situation keeps their dice, and all other players remove 1 dice from their cup.

  9. The game ends once only one player is left standing, whom then is the loser.

  10. ones are wild, and if the dice are in order, starting from one, to the highest value a ladder is formed.

  11. A ladder is equal to the number of dice in the ladder plus 1. All values in a ladder are wild.

  12. An examples value which are greater than other values is as following.

  13. Five fives are greater than five fours.

  14. Six sixes are greater than five sixes.

  15. Ten twos are greater than eight fours.
